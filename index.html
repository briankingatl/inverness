The Neighborhood

An unhurried stretch of north Atlanta, kept that way on purpose.

Inverness was established in 1986 along a quiet ridge above the Chattahoochee, with the river bending wide just south of us. The neighborhood has matured into one of Roswell's most established addresses, known less for grandeur than for character and beauty.

The streets here take their names from what grows along them: Azalea, Camellia, Wisteria, Dogwood. The original plantings have had nearly forty years to do their work, and in April the canopy is so thick over Azalea Drive that afternoon light arrives in a green half-light, making the clubhouse seem to float in it.

Inverness is in fact three enclaves under one trust: the original Inverness section, laid out in 1986; Riverview, where the lots grow larger as they approach the river bluff; and Riverbluff, the newest of the three, with the highest ground and the longest views. Two hundred and sixty homes in all, with covenants written to keep the streetscape consistent without erasing the personality of any one house.

What sets Inverness apart is less what is inside it than what is beside it. The Chattahoochee Nature Center is our next-door neighbor, and the river itself is a ten-minute walk through the woods. The historic Roswell square, with its mill ruins, Vickery Creek Trail, and variety of restaurants, is a fifteen-minute walk in the other direction. In a real sense, we are surrounded by the kind of amenities most neighborhoods in metro Atlanta can only dream of.

The Grounds

Secluded grounds, maintained for resident enjoyment.

Secluded within our beautifully maintained grounds are the clubhouse, pool, tennis courts, and playground. Whether you're taking laps in the five‑lane pool, joining a match on one of four lit courts, or hosting friends in the clubhouse, the amenities offer a relaxed, resort‑like atmosphere for residents and their guests. All facilities are maintained by the Trust and are available from dawn until 10 p.m.

No. 01 · The Clubhouse

The Clubhouse

Located at 2072 Azalea Drive, the clubhouse was renovated in 2019 and features a spanning glass view overlooking the pool and surrounding grounds. Inside you'll find a vaulted gathering room with a welcoming fireplace, a fully equipped caterer's kitchen, and flexible seating suitable for social events and private rentals. Residents may book the clubhouse through the resident portal.

No. 02 · The Pool

The Swimming Pool

The pool offers five swim lanes plus a shallow area for children. It is typically open from Memorial Day through Labor Day, with lifeguards on duty during peak hours. The surrounding terrace accommodates lounge seating and shaded areas for relaxing between swims. The community swim team, the Inverness Stingrays, practices and competes each season.

No. 03 · The Courts

Tennis

Four courts provide ample space for league play and casual matches. One court is marked for pickleball and can also accommodate informal basketball play. The courts were resurfaced in 2022 and include new fencing and a shaded viewing pergola. A regular Saturday morning doubles group plays year‑round, and courts may be reserved through the resident portal.

A shaded playground adjacent to the pool area, featuring a climbing structure, swings, and a small pavilion with picnic seating. The playground is intended for neighborhood use and is open from dawn until dusk; families should supervise young children while on the equipment.

No. 05 · Nature & the River

Nature & the River

The Chattahoochee Nature Center borders Inverness directly, and the river itself is a ten-minute walk through the woods behind the neighborhood. A network of trails, including the Vickery Creek Trail, connects residents to the riverwalk and the historic mill ruins in downtown Roswell. Deer, herons, and other wildlife are regular visitors to the wooded lots along the property line.
